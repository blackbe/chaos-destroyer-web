## 2. Grounding Checklist (Review Before Sending)

Before you send any message, pause and check:

- [ ] Am I calm right now? (Not anxious, angry, or desperate)
- [ ] Is this message about HER, not about MY pain?
- [ ] Does it require a response? (It shouldn't)
- [ ] Is there any guilt, pressure, or "why won't you..." energy?
- [ ] Would I be okay if she never responds to this?
- [ ] Have I waited at least 2 weeks since my last message?

**If any box is unchecked, wait. Rewrite. Or don't send.**

## 3. Pre-Written Message Templates

### Random Check-Ins (Use every 2-4 weeks)
> Hey Delilah, just thinking about you. Hope you're doing okay.

> Hi Delilah. No need to reply — just wanted you to know I'm here.

> Hey, hope school/life is going alright. Thinking of you.

### Asking to Connect (Use sparingly)
> Hey Delilah, would you be open to a FaceTime call sometime? No pressure if not.

> I'd love to hear your voice if you're ever up for a quick call. No rush.

### Birthday (June)
> Happy birthday, Delilah. I hope you have a really good day. I love you and I'm proud of the person you're becoming.

### Holidays
> Happy [holiday]. Thinking about you today. Hope it's a good one.

### If She Responds (Stay calm, don't over-explain)
> Thanks for responding. It means a lot. I'm here whenever you want to talk — no pressure.

### If She's Angry or Accusatory (Don't defend, don't argue)
> I hear you. I'm not going to argue. I just want you to know I love you and I'm here when you're ready.

### If You Learn Messages Aren't Getting Through
> Hey Jess, I want to make sure my messages to Delilah are reaching her. Can you confirm she's seeing them? I'm not asking for anything — just want to know she knows I'm thinking of her.

## 5. Core Principles (From Your Therapist + Research)

1. **The goal is emotional safety, not reunification.** Pushing for visits backfires.

2. **Low-pressure signals of availability.** "I'm here" without "Why won't you..."

3. **Repair without defense.** Don't relitigate the past. Just acknowledge her experience.

4. **Respect her autonomy.** She's 14, autistic, and needs control over her own boundaries.

5. **Time + consistency.** This is measured in months and years, not days.

6. **Your calm is the message.** If you're dysregulated, she'll feel it even through text.

## 6. What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't ask "Why won't you talk to me?"
- ❌ Don't send long explanations or defenses
- ❌ Don't guilt-trip ("I'm so sad without you")
- ❌ Don't bad-mouth Jess to Delilah
- ❌ Don't demand responses
- ❌ Don't increase frequency when she doesn't respond
- ❌ Don't show up unannounced
- ❌ Don't use court/legal pressure as a threat

## 7. Signs of Progress (Celebrate Small Wins)

- She reads the message (even without responding)
- She responds with anything, even "ok"
- She doesn't block you
- She mentions you to others neutrally
- She accepts a call, even briefly
- She asks you a question

**Any of these is a win. Don't push for more.**
